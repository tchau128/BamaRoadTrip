# Alabama Road Trip
**Gulf shrimping culture & civil rights history — Huntsville loop**
July 19–24, 2026

## Route at a Glance
Huntsville → Cullman → Bayou La Batre / Dauphin Island → Mobile → Selma → Montgomery → Birmingham → Huntsville

## Daily Notes
- [[Day 1 - Sunday July 19]] — Depart Huntsville, Ave Maria Grotto, backroads south, arrive Bayou La Batre
- [[Day 2 - Monday July 20]] — Bamahenge, shrimp docks, Africatown, overnight Mobile
- [[Day 3 - Tuesday July 21]] — Selma (Edmund Pettus Bridge, Voting Rights Museum), on to Montgomery
- [[Day 4 - Wednesday July 22]] — Legacy Museum, National Memorial, Freedom Monument Park, Rosa Parks Museum
- [[Day 5 - Thursday July 23]] — Birmingham: 16th Street Baptist Church, Civil Rights Institute, Sloss Furnaces
- [[Day 6 - Friday July 24]] — Cathedral Caverns, return to Huntsville

## Key Notes & Tips

**Shrimp boat tours**
Bayou La Batre has no formal tour desk — call local eco-tour/charter operators ahead, or walk the docks along Shell Belt Road when boats come in (mid-morning).

**Legacy Museum & Memorial**
Closed Tuesdays. Timed entry tickets required — book online in advance, they can sell out.

**Boiled peanut stands**
Look for honor-system roadside stands on backroads through the Black Belt (old Hwy 43, Hwy 84, near Camden/Grove Hill). Sunday's drive is prime territory.

**Bamahenge detour**
Adds roughly 2.5–3 hrs round trip from Bayou La Batre. Easiest thing to cut if Monday feels too packed.

**Cathedral Caverns**
Cave tours run hourly starting 10:15 AM in summer (90 min, book ahead). Leave Birmingham early to make the first tour.
