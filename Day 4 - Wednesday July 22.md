# Day 4 — Wednesday, July 22

⬅ [[Day 3 - Tuesday July 21|Day 3]] | ➡ [[Day 5 - Thursday July 23|Day 5]]

**Theme:** Retrace the 1965 Selma-to-Montgomery voting rights march — west to east, the way the marchers walked it, along US-80 (the Selma to Montgomery National Historic Trail).

**Weather logic:** Selma is drier in the morning (45%), Montgomery climbs to 70% by afternoon. So: outdoor/symbolic sites in Selma while it's drier → indoor museums in Montgomery when the rain picks up.

## ⚠️ Book tonight
- [ ] **Legacy Museum timed-entry ticket** — afternoon slot. Books up in advance, can sell out. Doing Selma first, so give yourself a relaxed morning and a mid-to-late afternoon entry.

## Morning — Selma (drier window)

### Edmund Pettus Bridge
- Walk it — site of Bloody Sunday, March 7, 1965
- Best done before Montgomery's afternoon rain odds climb
- Bring rain gear regardless — showers come and go

### National Voting Rights Museum & Institute
- Right at the foot of the bridge

## Midday — Drive US-80 east to Montgomery (~50 min)
- This IS the march route — the Selma to Montgomery National Historic Trail
- ~50 miles due east through the Black Belt

## Afternoon — Montgomery Legacy Sites (indoor, rain-proof)

### The Legacy Museum
- **Timed entry — book ahead (see above)**
- Marchers ended at the state capitol steps; you're arriving where the march arrived

### National Memorial for Peace and Justice
- Free shuttle from the museum
- Outdoor — time it for a dry gap in the showers
- Budget 1–2 hours

### Rosa Parks Museum & Civil Rights Memorial Center
- Both indoor — good rainy-afternoon backstops

### Dexter Avenue King Memorial Baptist Church & Alabama Capitol (exterior)
- Walkable downtown; the capitol steps are where the march concluded
